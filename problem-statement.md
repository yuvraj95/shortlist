# Problem Statement — Shortlist

## Context

Job searching in 2025 is a volume problem masquerading as a discovery problem. The tools exist. The jobs are out there. But the experience of finding the right one is still fundamentally broken for most people.

---

## The Four Core Pains

**1. Volume without signal**
Job boards return hundreds of postings per search query. Most are irrelevant. There is no objective indicator of how well a given posting actually matches the seeker's resume, skills, or experience level. Candidates are left to manually read through dozens of JDs to figure out if they are even a reasonable fit.

**2. No memory**
Hidden or dismissed jobs reappear on every visit. Every session starts from scratch. Users repeat the same triage work over and over — a fundamental UX failure that wastes significant time for active job seekers.

**3. Fragmentation**
LinkedIn, Indeed, Wellfound, and company career pages each require their own search. There is no unified view. A job seeker running a serious search has to maintain parallel workflows across multiple platforms, each with different interfaces, filters, and data freshness.

**4. No fit transparency**
Even when a job looks relevant on the surface, there is no way to quickly assess alignment between the posting requirements and the candidate's actual background without reading every line of both documents.

---

## Target Users

| Persona | Description | Primary Need |
|---|---|---|
| Active seeker | Senior IC or PM applying to 5-15 roles per week | Signal-rich filtering, persistent triage, no repeated noise |
| Passive browser | Employed professional scanning the market periodically | Quick market view without the friction of a lengthy setup |

---

## What Better Looks Like

A personal job discovery tool that:
- Lets users sign up instantly and upload a resume to get a scored feed in seconds
- Scores every posting against the user's actual resume — objectively and transparently
- Remembers every hide and save decision so the feed gets cleaner over time
- Offers a fast resume-independent search for casual market browsing
- Replaces the noise of generic job board scrolling with a focused, signal-rich experience

---

## Hypothesis

If a job seeker can see a clear match score against their resume, filter out noise permanently, and search across sources in one place — they will spend significantly less time on triage and more time on applications that actually fit.
